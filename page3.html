EGG NUTRITION AND FACTS

Did you know that eggs are rich in protein and essential vitamins? They contain Vitamin B2, Vitamin D, Vitamin A, and more.

Eggs are also a great source of choline, which is vital for brain health and development.

One large egg contains about 6 grams of high-quality protein, which helps in muscle repair and growth.

Eggs contain antioxidants like lutein and zeaxanthin, which are important for eye health and may help reduce the risk of cataracts.

Eggs are one of the few foods that naturally contain Vitamin D, supporting bone health and immune function.

Despite their cholesterol content, research shows that eggs do not significantly raise cholesterol levels in most people.

Including eggs in your diet can help with weight management, as they are filling and can reduce overall calorie intake when eaten for breakfast.
